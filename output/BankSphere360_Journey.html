01 — The Brief

What we are building, and why a bank

A bank is the perfect teaching domain: it has money flowing in (deposits), money flowing out (loans), risk that must be watched (NPAs), and customers behaving across channels. Every Power BI concept has a natural home here.

BankSphere 360 consolidates core-banking, customer-master and transaction data into one governed semantic model and serves five personas — leadership, branch heads, risk officers, relationship managers and compliance — through three role-secured dashboards. It replaces hand-built spreadsheet packs with a single certified source of truth, refreshed daily.

DAY 05DAX FoundationsContext

Two contexts: row (current row, iterators) and filter (slicers in effect). CALCULATE is the only function that modifies filter context.

Measure vs calculated column: measures compute at query time (KPIs); columns compute at refresh and are stored (slicing/keys).

Hands-on
Total Deposits =
  CALCULATE( SUM(FactAccounts[Balance]),
            DimProduct[ProductCategory] = "Deposits" )

NPA % = DIVIDE( [NPA Amount], [Total Loan Book] )
Interview hook

"SUM vs SUMX?" → SUM aggregates a column; SUMX iterates row-by-row evaluating an expression.

DAY 06Advanced DAX & PerformanceTime Intel

Time-intelligence (TOTALYTD, SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR, DATEADD), ranking (RANKX), and VAR for readability + speed.

Hands-on
Txn YoY % = DIVIDE( [Total Txn Value] - [Txn Value LY], [Txn Value LY] )

Txn MoM % =
  VAR Cur  = [Total Txn Value]
  VAR Prev = CALCULATE([Total Txn Value], DATEADD(DimDate[Date],-1,MONTH))
  RETURN DIVIDE(Cur - Prev, Prev)

04 — The Star Schema

How the model fits together

Four conformed dimensions (teal) feed three fact tables (gold). One date table drives every time-based metric — that is what makes slicing by month, quarter or year consistent everywhere.

  • ProductID 🔑

FactAccounts
  • AccountID 🔑
  • Balance, Status
  • InterestRate
  • FK: Customer/Product/Branch/Date
FactTransactions
  • TransactionID 🔑
  • Amount, Channel
  • TxnType, IsFraudFlag
  • FK: Account, Date
FactLoans
  • LoanID 🔑
  • OutstandingAmount, EMI
  • DPD, NPAFlag, Status

DimBranch

Design decisions. No snowflaking (simpler DAX, faster queries). One active relationship between any two tables. DimDate marked as a date table. Foreign keys hidden from the report field list.

06 — The Interview

Technical, Managerial & HR — answered

Click each question to reveal a concise, defensible answer. Rehearse out loud. The full bank lives in NOTES.md.

A · Technical Round

Import vs DirectQuery vs Composite — when each?
Import copies data into VertiPaq: fastest, full DAX, scheduled refresh — the default. DirectQuery leaves queries in the source for very large or near-real-time data, at the cost of speed and some DAX/PQ limits. Composite mixes both, often with aggregations: detail in DirectQuery, summaries imported.
Explain row context vs filter context.
Row context is "the current row" — it exists in calculated columns and inside iterators like SUMX. Filter context is the set of filters applied by slicers, rows/columns of a visual, and CALCULATE. CALCULATE can convert row context into filter context (context transition), which is why a measure behaves differently inside an iterator.
What does CALCULATE do?
It evaluates an expression in a filter context that you modify with its filter arguments — the only function that changes filter context. Example: NPA% = DIVIDE(CALCULATE(SUM(Outstanding), NPAFlag=1), [Total Loan Book]).
Star vs snowflake schema — and why star?
Star = denormalized dimensions directly around the facts. Snowflake = dimensions normalized into sub-tables. Star wins in Power BI: fewer joins, better VertiPaq compression, simpler and faster DAX, cleaner relationships.
What is query folding and how do you verify it?
Power Query translates your steps into native source queries (e.g., SQL) so the source does the heavy lifting — faster refresh, less memory. Verify with right-click step → View Native Query. Steps like adding index columns or certain custom logic can break folding, so do foldable steps first.
Measure vs calculated column vs calculated table?
Measure: computed at query time, no storage, aggregated — use for KPIs. Calculated column: computed at refresh, stored per row — use for slicing or relationships. Calculated table: a DAX-generated table (e.g., a date table or bridge).
How do you compute YoY growth?
SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR inside CALCULATE gives last year's value; then DIVIDE the difference: DIVIDE([Value]-[ValueLY],[ValueLY]). Requires a marked date table with continuous dates.
ALL vs ALLEXCEPT vs REMOVEFILTERS vs ALLSELECTED?
ALL removes all filters from a table/column. ALLEXCEPT removes all except the columns you list. REMOVEFILTERS is the clearer modern alias for ALL used as a CALCULATE modifier. ALLSELECTED respects the user's outer selections (slicers) while ignoring inner visual filters — useful for % of visible total.
Static vs dynamic RLS?
Static = a role per filter value (Region="West") — simple but doesn't scale. Dynamic = a user-to-attribute mapping table filtered by USERPRINCIPALNAME(), so one role serves everyone. Roles are defined in Desktop; members are assigned in the Service; admins bypass RLS by default.
A report is slow — how do you fix it?
Use Performance Analyzer to find the slow visual and its DAX duration. Common fixes: fewer visuals per page, lower cardinality, measures instead of calculated columns, boolean column filters instead of FILTER(table), restore query folding, enforce a star schema, and consider aggregations/composite models for big data.
SQL: top 5 branches by transaction value?
SELECT b.BranchName, SUM(t.Amount) v FROM FactTransactions t JOIN FactAccounts a ON t.AccountID=a.AccountID JOIN DimBranch b ON a.BranchID=b.BranchID GROUP BY b.BranchName ORDER BY v DESC LIMIT 5; Know WHERE (pre-aggregation) vs HAVING (post-aggregation) and window functions like ROW_NUMBER/RANK.
What is incremental refresh?
Partition a large fact table by date using RangeStart/RangeEnd parameters; Power BI refreshes only recent partitions instead of the whole table. The date filter must fold to the source. Dramatically cuts refresh time and load.

B · Managerial / Project Round

Walk me through a Power BI project end-to-end.
Use BankSphere 360: stakeholder workshops → five personas, eight KPIs frozen in an SSRD → profile SQL/ERP/Excel sources → Power Query ETL preserving folding → star schema with conformed dims → DAX KPIs incl. time intelligence → three pages with drillthrough & bookmarks → dynamic RLS → publish with gateway + scheduled refresh → deployment pipelines → runbook. Result: one certified source of truth, reporting effort cut from days to minutes.
How do you gather requirements and handle scope creep?
Persona workshops, written KPI definitions signed off in the SSRD, and a mock-up before building. New asks become logged change requests, estimated and prioritized — not silent additions. The signed KPI definitions are the anchor when scope drifts.
A stakeholder disputes a number — what do you do?
Stay calm and reproduce it. Check the measure's filter context, trace it back through the model, and reconcile against the source control totals. If the model is right, walk them through the definition; if it's wrong, hotfix in Dev, retest, and promote. Trust comes from transparency, not defensiveness.
How do you prioritize three teams wanting changes the same week?
Impact vs effort, against business value and risk. Regulatory/risk items (NPA accuracy) outrank cosmetic ones. I make the queue visible, communicate timelines, and batch low-effort items together.
Describe a performance problem you solved.
Frame it concretely: a page loaded slowly; Performance Analyzer pointed to a high-cardinality calculated column and a FILTER over a whole fact table. I moved the logic to a measure with boolean filters, reduced cardinality, and confirmed folding — load time dropped materially. Always quantify the before/after.

C · HR Round

Tell me about yourself.
A 90-second arc: years in BI and data, banking-domain depth, a signature project (BankSphere 360), the measurable value delivered, and where you want to grow (modern data/AI alongside BI). End forward-looking, tied to this role.
Why are you looking for a change?
Frame toward growth, never away from negativity: you want broader ownership of end-to-end BI and to deepen modern data/cloud skills, which this role offers. Keep it positive and specific.
What are your strengths and weaknesses?
Strength: translating fuzzy business questions into governed, performant models (evidence it). Weakness: pick a real, improving one — e.g., earlier you over-engineered before validating requirements, and now you mock up and confirm KPIs first. Show the correction.
Salary expectations?
Give a researched range anchored to market and your seniority, signal flexibility for the right overall package, and turn it into a conversation rather than a single number. Do the market research beforehand for the specific city/role.
Do you have questions for us?
Always yes. Ask about the team structure, the organization's data maturity and tooling, and what success in this role looks like at the 6-month mark. It shows you think like an owner.
